Early 2016, I then decided to move back to London Colney store. March 2017 - June 2021

In March 2017, I started working for the Warehouse, NFT Distribution (later to be renamed EV Cargo Logistics). I started this job in the ambient department, working night shift on picking until July 2017, where I was transferred to the chilled department on the day shift.

From July 2017 until June 2021, I worked in the Chilled department of NFT, and during my time there, I had been trained on many different jobs. The first job I was trained in, was picking, which involved working with my collagues in each grid to make sure each product went to the correct shop, keeping the roll cages stacked nicely.

The second job I was trained on in the chilled department, was goods in, which involved making sure each product was seperated individually, and making sure the quantity recieved for each product was correct, before sending it off to be picked by pickers. After being trained on goods in, I often found myself helping others to seperate each product, so that they can count how much had been recieved of each product.

I had then been trained on Marshalling, which was the task I found myself doing the majority of the time for the rest of my NFT career. This involved sorting each completed cage into locations, making it easier for loaders to find these cages for loading. This task proved to be very difficult on busy days, as there was only ever a maximum of 4 marshallers on shift at any one time, which would always depend on the volume inbound.

Another job that I occassionally found myself doing, is grid control, which involved keeping each of my assigned grids clean and tidy for goods in to have more space to do their task, whilst also allowing pickers to pick a product quicker. When there was nothing to do whilst on grid control, I would find myself helping the goods in staff to make sure everything was completed as quickly as possible.

The final job I was trained on, was tipping. Tipping was another job from the goods in department, which simply involved unloading trailers, and bringing each pallet to their assigned grids.

During my time at NFT, I was trained to drive LLOP trucks to work in the ambient department when required, and to do Marshalling in the chilled department. I was also trained on the PPT truck to help with tipping. Towards the end of my career, I would also sometimes help loaders load their trailers, by bringing roll cages to the bays that they needed to go to.

Skills I think could be useful from this job
